About the Project

Understanding global migration through human rights analysis.

Mission

The Migration Ledger is a student-led research initiative exploring the intersection of human rights and migration policy through comparative case studies. Through accessible, evidence-based articles and visual data presentations, this project analyzes migration systems and human rights protections across nations—offering insight into how policy reflects our shared global responsibility.

About the Founder

Neil Adi is a student at Broughton High School in Raleigh, North Carolina, with a deep interest in international relations, political science, and the ethical dimensions of policy. After ten years in a Spanish immersion program, Neil is fluent in Spanish and focused on cross-cultural communication and global perspectives.

His academic strengths lie in the social sciences and biology, with a growing interest in the intersection of policy, ethics, and data. Beyond the classroom, Neil is co-founder of the Congressional Debate Branch, a member of Model UN, co-chair of the Student Equity Team, and treasurer of the Environmental Club—roles that reflect a commitment to advocacy, research, and collaboration.

The Migration Ledger grew out of a desire to better understand how nations balance sovereignty, security, and human dignity—and to make that analysis accessible to others who share that curiosity.
